Supporting Long-Term Recovery

Recovery is a lifelong journey that extends far beyond initial treatment. ThreadSure provides comprehensive recovery support services that help individuals maintain their wellbeing, build fulfilling lives, and navigate the challenges of long-term recovery. Our peer-led and professional support systems create communities of mutual aid and personal growth.

Our Recovery Support Philosophy

Peer-Led Support

Experienced peers providing guidance and understanding based on lived experience.

Holistic Wellbeing

Supporting physical, mental, emotional, and social aspects of recovery.

Community Building

Creating supportive networks that foster belonging and mutual aid.

Recovery Support Services

Peer Support Networks

Experienced individuals in recovery providing guidance, mentorship, and emotional support to others on their journey.

Includes:

  • • One-to-one peer mentoring
  • • Peer support groups
  • • Recovery coaching
  • • Telephone support lines
  • • Online peer communities

Mutual Aid Groups

Self-help groups where individuals support each other through shared experiences and collective wisdom.

Includes:

  • • 12-step fellowships
  • • SMART Recovery meetings
  • • Women for Sobriety
  • • Secular Organizations for Sobriety
  • • Refuge Recovery

Recovery Coaching

Professional coaching to help individuals develop skills, set goals, and navigate challenges in recovery.

Includes:

  • • Goal setting and planning
  • • Skills development
  • • Relapse prevention strategies
  • • Lifestyle planning
  • • Ongoing motivation support

Recovery Communities

Structured communities providing long-term support, social activities, and recovery-focused events.

Includes:

  • • Recovery cafes and centers
  • • Social and recreational activities
  • • Recovery housing communities
  • • Alumni networks
  • • Family and community events

Benefits of Peer Support

Understanding & Empathy

Peers understand the challenges of recovery because they've experienced them personally.

  • • Shared lived experience
  • • Non-judgmental support
  • • Practical coping strategies
  • • Emotional validation

Hope & Motivation

Seeing others succeed in recovery provides hope and demonstrates that change is possible.

  • • Role model inspiration
  • • Realistic expectations
  • • Achievement celebration
  • • Ongoing encouragement

Community & Belonging

Recovery communities provide a sense of belonging and reduce feelings of isolation.

  • • Social connection
  • • Shared identity
  • • Mutual support
  • • Collective strength
